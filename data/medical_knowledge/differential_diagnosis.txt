MEDICAL KNOWLEDGE BASE - DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS BY SYMPTOM
Source: Clinical Decision Support Reference (Educational)

=============================================================
FEVER - DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS
=============================================================

Fever (<38.3°C - Low Grade):
- Most likely: Viral URTI (common cold), early viral infection, dehydration, post-vaccination reaction
- Less likely: Early bacterial infection, inflammatory condition
- Management: Monitor, hydration, antipyretics if uncomfortable

Fever (38.3-39°C - Moderate):
- Most likely: Influenza, COVID-19, bacterial infection (UTI, sinusitis, pharyngitis), viral illness
- Less likely: Early pneumonia, infectious mononucleosis
- Management: Antipyretics, evaluate for source, consider testing

Fever (>39°C - High):
- Urgent consideration: Bacterial pneumonia, sepsis, meningitis, urinary tract infection, severe influenza
- Red flag: Fever + stiff neck + photophobia = Meningitis until proven otherwise (EMERGENCY)
- Management: Urgent medical evaluation, blood cultures if sepsis suspected

APPROACH TO FEVER WITH KEY ASSOCIATED SYMPTOMS:
- Fever + sore throat + lymphadenopathy → Pharyngitis/tonsillitis (Group A strep), Mono
- Fever + productive cough + chest pain → Pneumonia
- Fever + dysuria + flank pain → Pyelonephritis (kidney infection)
- Fever + headache + stiff neck → Meningitis (EMERGENCY)
- Fever + rash (non-blanching) → Meningococcemia (EMERGENCY)
- Fever + joint pain + rash → Septic arthritis, Lyme disease, viral arthritis
- Fever + diarrhea + vomiting → Gastroenteritis
- Fever + confusion → Sepsis, encephalitis, severe infection (HIGH RISK)

CHEST PAIN - DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS
=============================================================

CARDIAC (HIGH RISK - Always consider first):
- Acute Myocardial Infarction (STEMI/NSTEMI): Central, crushing, radiates to arm/jaw, diaphoresis, nausea
- Unstable Angina: Similar to MI but may be transient/with exertion
- Pericarditis: Sharp, worse lying flat, improved leaning forward, friction rub

PULMONARY:
- Pulmonary Embolism: Sudden pleuritic chest pain, dyspnea, tachycardia, risk factors (immobility, cancer)
- Pneumothorax: Sudden sharp pain, shortness of breath, decreased breath sounds unilaterally
- Pleuritis: Sharp, worse with breathing, positional

GASTROINTESTINAL:
- GERD/Esophagitis: Burning, epigastric, after meals, lying down, relieved by antacids
- Esophageal Spasm: Can mimic cardiac, colicky, associated with swallowing
- Peptic Ulcer: Epigastric, may relate to meals

MUSCULOSKELETAL:
- Costochondritis: Reproducible tenderness on palpation, sharp, positional
- Muscle strain: Clear trauma/overuse history, tenderness

PSYCHIATRIC:
- Panic Attack: Palpitations, sweating, fear, dyspnea, no ECG changes — diagnosis of exclusion

RISK STRATIFICATION FOR CHEST PAIN:
- ALWAYS treat as cardiac until proven otherwise
- Any chest pain with dyspnea, diaphoresis, radiation → HIGH RISK, EMERGENCY
- Young patient, clear musculoskeletal trigger, reproducible on palpation → Lower risk but still evaluate

=============================================================
SHORTNESS OF BREATH - DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS
=============================================================

ACUTE ONSET:
- Pulmonary Embolism: Sudden, pleuritic chest pain, tachycardia, risk factors
- Pneumothorax: Sudden, unilateral decreased breath sounds
- Anaphylaxis: Allergy exposure, urticaria, angioedema
- Acute Pulmonary Edema: Pink frothy sputum, orthopnea, cardiac history
- Acute Severe Asthma: Wheezing, known asthma, triggers

SUBACUTE/CHRONIC:
- Asthma: Episodic, wheezing, reversible, triggers
- COPD: Chronic smoker, progressive dyspnea, chronic cough
- Heart Failure: Exertional dyspnea, orthopnea, edema, cardiac history
- Pneumonia: Fever, productive cough, pleuritic pain
- Anemia: Fatigue, pallor, tachycardia, exertional dyspnea

=============================================================
HEADACHE - DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS
=============================================================

PRIMARY HEADACHES:
- Tension Headache: Bilateral, pressing/band-like, mild-moderate, not worsened by activity
- Migraine: Unilateral, throbbing, moderate-severe, nausea, photo/phonophobia, may have aura
- Cluster Headache: Unilateral periorbital, severe, autonomic features (watering eye, nasal congestion)

SECONDARY HEADACHES (DANGEROUS - Red Flags):
- Subarachnoid Hemorrhage: "Thunderclap" - sudden, severe "worst headache of life"
- Meningitis: Fever, stiff neck, photophobia, progressive
- Hypertensive Crisis: BP >180/120, associated neurological symptoms
- Brain Tumor: Progressive, worse in morning, associated with neurological deficits
- Giant Cell Arteritis: Age >50, temporal tenderness, jaw claudication, visual changes

ASSESSMENT APPROACH (SNOOP4 Red Flags):
S - Systemic symptoms (fever, weight loss)
N - Neurological symptoms or signs
O - Onset sudden or explosive
O - Older age (>50, new headache pattern)
P - Previous headache history (change in pattern)
P - Postural change worsens
P - Progressive worsening
P - Papilledema (sign of raised intracranial pressure)

ABDOMINAL PAIN - DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS BY LOCATION
=============================================================

RIGHT UPPER QUADRANT (RUQ):
- Cholecystitis: Sharp/colicky, after fatty meals, fever, Murphy's sign positive
- Hepatitis: Dull ache, jaundice, anorexia, dark urine
- Pleuritic pain referred from right lung base pneumonia

RIGHT LOWER QUADRANT (RLQ):
- Appendicitis: Periumbilical pain migrating to RLQ, fever, anorexia, rebound tenderness
- Ovarian pathology (women): Ovarian cyst, torsion, ectopic pregnancy
- Inguinal hernia, mesenteric adenitis

EPIGASTRIC:
- Peptic Ulcer Disease: Burning, relation to meals (worse/better depending on H. pylori vs NSAID)
- GERD: Burning, post-prandial
- Pancreatitis: Severe, radiating to back, nausea, alcohol history
- MI: Can present as epigastric pain (ALWAYS consider cardiac)

LEFT UPPER QUADRANT (LUQ):
- Splenic pathology, gastric pathology, pancreatitis (tail)
- Left lower lobe pneumonia (referred)

LEFT LOWER QUADRANT (LLQ):
- Diverticulitis: Older patient, fever, LLQ pain
- Ovarian pathology (women)
- IBD (Crohn's/Ulcerative Colitis): Chronic, diarrhea, rectal bleeding

DIFFUSE ABDOMINAL PAIN:
- Gastroenteritis: Cramping, diffuse, diarrhea, vomiting
- Peritonitis: Rigid abdomen, guarding, rebound - EMERGENCY
- Bowel Obstruction: Colicky, distension, constipation, vomiting
- Mesenteric Ischemia: Severe pain out of proportion to exam, risk factors

FATIGUE - DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS
=============================================================

COMMON CAUSES:
- Sleep deprivation/poor sleep hygiene
- Anemia (iron deficiency, B12 deficiency)
- Hypothyroidism
- Depression
- Diabetes mellitus
- Chronic infection (viral)
- Dehydration

LESS COMMON BUT IMPORTANT:
- Adrenal insufficiency
- Heart failure
- Chronic kidney disease
- Malignancy (weight loss, night sweats, lymphadenopathy)
- Sleep apnea
- Autoimmune conditions

FATIGUE ASSESSMENT:
- Duration: Acute (<1 month) vs. chronic (>6 months)
- Associated symptoms: Weight loss, night sweats, fever (consider malignancy, chronic infection)
- Impact on function: Mild vs. severe (functional impairment)
- Timing: Worse in morning (depression, hypothyroidism) vs. worse evening (normal tiredness, anemia)

=============================================================
DERMATOLOGICAL SYMPTOMS - KEY PATTERNS
=============================================================

RASH WITH FEVER:
- Meningococcal: Non-blanching petechiae/purpura → EMERGENCY
- Viral exanthem: Maculopapular, blanching, starts trunk
- Drug reaction: After new medication, variable morphology
- Varicella (Chickenpox): Vesicular, pruritic, starts head/trunk
- Measles: Koplik's spots, maculopapular starting face
- Lyme Disease: Target-shaped (erythema migrans) after tick bite

RASH WITHOUT FEVER:
- Contact Dermatitis: Localized, exposure history, pruritic
- Urticaria (Hives): Pruritic wheals, possible allergic trigger
- Eczema/Atopic Dermatitis: Chronic, pruritic, typical distribution (flexures)
- Psoriasis: Silvery plaques, extensor surfaces, scalp, nails

=============================================================
URINARY SYMPTOMS - DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS
=============================================================

DYSURIA (Painful Urination) + FREQUENCY:
- Urinary Tract Infection (UTI): Most common in women, urgency, frequency, suprapubic pain, ± fever
- Urethritis (STI): Consider in sexually active, discharge
- Cystitis: Bladder infection, no upper tract involvement
- Pyelonephritis: UTI + fever + flank/loin pain → Moderate-High risk

INCREASED URINARY FREQUENCY WITHOUT DYSURIA:
- Diabetes mellitus: Polyuria + polydipsia
- Diabetes insipidus: Large volumes, dilute
- Prostatism (BPH in men): Nocturia, poor stream, hesitancy
- Overactive bladder: Urgency without infection

HEMATURIA (Blood in Urine):
- Microscopic: UTI, stone, glomerulonephritis, malignancy
- Macroscopic (visible blood): Kidney/bladder stone, UTI, malignancy (painless hematuria in older patients → investigate for malignancy)
